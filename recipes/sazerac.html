Sazerac

The Sazerac offers a unique flavor experience for rye whiskey. The recipe is simple, accenting the spicy rye with sugar, bitters, and an anise liqueur, such as absinthe. That last ingredient makes this drink special, and it's even better with the new crop of amazing ryes on the market today

Ingredients

  • Absinthe, to rinse
  • 1 sugar cube
  • 1/2 teaspoon cold water
  • 3 dashes Peychaud's bitters
  • A2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 11/4 ounces rye whiskey
  • 11/4 ounces cognac
  • Garnish: lemon peel

Steps

  1. Rinse a chilled rocks glass with absinthe, discarding any excess, and set aside.
  2. In a mixing glass, muddle the sugar cube, water and the Peychaud's and Angostura bitters.
  3. Add the rye and cognac, fill the mixing glass with ice and stir until well-chilled
  4. Strain into the prepared glass.
  5. Twist the lemon peel over the drink's surgace to express the peel's oils, then garnish with the peel.
